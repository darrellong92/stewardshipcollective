Who We Are

"A flourishing community reimagining, and joyfully stewarding His resources, to participate in Christ's redemptive work."

Stewardship Collective is a faith-based intermediary building trusted community between Christian wealth stewards and impact partners across Singapore and Southeast Asia. We are a convener, a bridge builder, and a community-maker — not a fund, not a platform.

As Christians, all our lives are lived in response to this profound act of humility and grace. In every season — in want and in plenty — we return to the image of the humble servant who emptied himself for us. That posture shapes everything: how we hold what we have, how we give, and how we show up for one another.

We continue to wrestle with the immense brokenness in this world. And yet we also see, again and again, that God's redemptive plan is constantly at work — moving through ordinary people, in ordinary places, doing ordinary things faithfully. He invites us to participate joyfully and sacrificially in the diverse roles He has called each of us to play.

Stewardship Collective was born from this tension — the tension between what is and what could be, between the brokenness we see and the redemption we trust is coming. It is our prayerful, humble attempt to propose one way forward for believers who want to hold their resources more faithfully, more relationally, and more joyfully.

We offer this work not as an answer, but as an invitation to discern together — with open hands.

The Problem We Are Responding To

God's resources are here.
The conditions to deploy them faithfully are not.

Across Singapore and Southeast Asia, there is no shortage of generosity or goodwill. What is missing is the trusted, relational infrastructure that allows resources to flow from the right hands to the right places — in ways that honour both the giver and the receiver.

The Steward

Wanting to give well. Often journeying alone.

  • Giving happens in isolation, without trusted peers to journey with
  • Biblical formation around stewardship is fragmented or absent
  • Limited awareness of where real needs are and how to engage meaningfully
  • Generosity can feel transactional — a cheque rather than a relationship
The Gap
The Partner

Doing important work. Chronically under-resourced.

  • Funding arrives without the strategic or relational support to use it well
  • Leaders burn out managing donor relationships alongside their core work
  • Significant human and financial capital remains unmobilised across SEA
  • No trusted intermediary connects both worlds with integrity and care
Our Vision & Role

We exist to close the gap — not with a programme, but with a community.

Stewardship Collective cultivates a Christ-centred community that reimagines how God's people steward His resources. We believe stewardship is not merely about allocation — it is about formation. Resources should flow from relationships. Capital should be paired with care. And giving should be joyful, not cautious.

"Generosity becomes cautious instead of joyful. Giving becomes a transaction instead of a transformation. And capacity gaps become chronic rather than redemptive."

We are not another fund or giving platform. We are a convener — bringing stewards and partners into genuine, trusted relationship. A bridge builder — creating pathways for resources to flow wisely and well. A community-maker — because the real work of stewardship happens in relationship, not in isolation.

How We Work

Three streams. One integrated model.

Everything SC does flows through three interconnected streams. They are not separate programmes — they are one way of working, each stream reinforcing the others.

I
Trusted Communities of Stewards
Stewardship Circles

We convene small, cause-aligned circles where stewards can share openly — including their joys, questions, and longings around giving — and learn from one another as peers. Formation comes before activation. Relationship comes before transaction.

These are not networking events or giving programmes. They are ongoing communities of practice — spaces for honest journeying together, grounded in Scripture and shaped by trust.

Monthly gatherings, discipleship-focused, built around a shared sense of call and cause.

II
Rigorous Deployment of Capital
Partner Funding

We create professional, excellent pathways for pooled giving — channelling resources through vetted, well-assessed partner initiatives. We don't simply pass money through. We stay present: linking funding with strategic support, ensuring capital reflects an abundance mindset rather than scarcity or passivity.

Every giving pathway is designed to honour both the steward's intent and the partner's dignity. Resources flow from relationship, not from a transaction.

Partners are assessed for trustworthiness, need, and readiness — not just programmatic outputs.

III
Capacity Building Beyond the Cheque
Partner Capacity

Strategic, relational, and practical support for on-the-ground partners — going far beyond funding. We walk alongside organisations in leadership development, organisational systems, fundraising capability, and long-term accompaniment.

Partners are not recipients of charity. They are collaborators in the same redemptive work. Our support is designed to strengthen their capacity to flourish — long after our involvement has ended.

Support is holistic and relational — not transactional or time-limited to a grant cycle.

Who We Serve

Three communities.
One ecosystem.

SC sits at the intersection of three communities, each with a distinct role in the stewardship ecosystem we are trying to build. The health of each depends on the others.

For stewards

Those who hold resources and want to give well

Individuals, families, and organisations with financial, relational, or organisational capacity. People who want to give generously and faithfully — but are looking for the community, formation, and trusted pathways to do so with integrity and joy.

For impact partners

Organisations doing justice and mercy work in SEA

Nonprofits, ministries, and social enterprises working on the ground across Singapore and Southeast Asia. Organisations that need more than funding — they need strategic accompaniment, capacity support, and genuine relationship with those who give.

For the wider church

The body of Christ, learning to hold things more loosely

Churches, pastors, and Christian communities wrestling with what faithful stewardship looks like in a context of growing wealth. We provide teaching, exposure journeys, and pathways for the broader church to participate in this work.
